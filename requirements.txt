Website Refresh – One-Page Outline
Objective
Refresh the DirectShadesAndBlinds.com website to feel modern, clean, and current, while clearly communicating who we are, 
the scale of our work, and our value as a national commercial window-covering partner. 
The site should emphasize projects, mill partnerships, and credibility, without being text-heavy.

Overall Direction
Clean, modern commercial aesthetic

Strong use of project photography

Simple navigation (6–7 main pages max)

Mobile-first and fast loading

Clear calls-to-action for bid requests

Recommended Site Structure


1. Home
Strong hero image or short video of commercial projects  (This is important.)
After the media section I want the next part of the webpage to scrollover the media when client scrolls down.

Clear positioning statement (national commercial window coverings)

Primary CTA: Request a Bid  (On top of media )

Secondary CTA: View Projects (On top of media )

Brief services overview  (Make it slide 2)

Featured projects (3–4) (make it slide 2)

Mill partnership highlight (Make it Slide 3)

Trust indicators (markets served, partners, or logos if approved) (Make this a loop that has 20 ceritficates that slides from left to right in infinity mode)

Short company overview and story

High-level leadership/team introduction

What differentiates Direct Shades & Blinds:

National reach

Mill-direct sourcing

Proven commercial execution

3. Services
Commercial window coverings

Project types: multifamily, hospitality, healthcare, office

Turnkey execution from bid through installation

Motorization / automation (high level)

4. Projects
Project gallery

Individual project pages with:

Project name & location

Developer & GC

Scope of work

Photos

(Optional) Current projects section

5. Mill & Manufacturing Partnerships
Overview of mill-direct relationships

Quality control and sourcing advantages
